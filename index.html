I am a first year Pre-Business student at the University of California Riverside. I oneday hope to work as a Real Estate Salesperson at a prominent brokerage making good commission, or working in Wall Street with my Finance degree that I am hoping to get after my four years here. Not only am I a passionate student willing to learn new things everyday, but I am determined to be successful no matter where life takes me. I have learned through working part time jobs, volunteering at homeless shelters and retirement homes, that nothing in life comes easy. I worked at a local restaurant called Boudin and was a server, busboy, and cashier. My first restaurant experience allowed me to realize what a blessing it was to simply live under a roof and be able to buy myself food. I also worked at a local milk tea shop called Gong Cha where I was a barista as well as a cashier.

Both my part time jobs allowed me to grow in ways that any other experience would not have allowed me to do. I hope to take these experiences with me throughout my lifetime when I apply for real full time jobs and am earning a salary or in my case of being a real estate agent, a commission. Being a real estate agent comes a level of hustle that no other job requires. As real estate agents do not get paid a steady salary, they are forced to work around the clock and look for potential buyers or seller leads at every hour. This sort of hustle is what I want from my job and how I want to be rewarded with my commission gives me the sense of me doing something right.

With me being a first year student, people may think I still have a long way to go before I enter the workforce, but I plan to enter it as soon as I can. I plan to obtain my real estate license over the summer and hopefully work by my second year as a part time real estate salesperson. I want exposure and time in the marketplace and I feel college is the perfect opportunity to obtain this. I will be able to acquire exposure in the marketplace through a mentor that I can ride with after I obtain my license. This will allow me to follow in the steps of an already successful real estate agent and allow me to gain experience as well as guidance into what it takes to be a good and successful agent in this competitive marketplace.
